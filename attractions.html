Attractions

Cities

Africa has some of the most beautiful cities in world located along the coastlines and inland. Capetown, located in South Africa, has amazing beaches, beautiful surrounding landscapes, an abundance of restaurants for delicious dining and a vibrant nightlife.
Cairo, Egypt's Capitol, is home to the great pyramids, one of our worlds great wonders. It also has the oldest and largest film and music industry in the Arab world. With it's exciting life, ancient history, museums and excursions, Cairo is certainly a city not to miss out on while visiting Africa.
Stone Town, located in beautiful Zanzibar, has to be one the most underrated cities int he world. Commonly referred to as "Spice Island", it has vast cinnamon, nutmeg, cardamom and clove plantations. The city is vibrant with color, festivities and beautiful shorelines surrounded by the Indian Ocean

Excursions

Africa is an incredible continent for adventurers. The countless amount of activities makes it a prime destination for traveling enthusiests. Safaries give visitors a chance to get close up view of some of the most endangered species in the world while gaining knowledge of their environment.
Kilimanjaro is an incredible tourist attraction for the avid hiker, such as myself. With a height of over 18,000ft, multiple trail routes and stunning views from the top there's nothing more could you ask for in an adventure.
Hot air ballooning has become incredibly popular with African tourists. It offers tourists the greatest arial views of Africa's beautiful landscapes and wildlife giving them a truly unforgettable experience.
Madagascar Island is home to some of the rarest animal and plant life on the planet. Although tourists are seeking after it, it is crucial that the government and tour guides limit the number of people impacting the habitats. The beautiful, lush jungles and surrounding Indian Ocean are homes to animal and plant life unable to exist in any other environment or climate making Madagascar an incredible addition to Africa.

Landscapes

With its endless beaches, unending mountain jungles, hidden waterfalls and vast deserts, Africa has some the most beautiful landscapes carved out by mother nature over centuries. Victoria Falls, located on the border of Zamibia and Zimbabwe, is one of the worlds largest waterfalls with a width of over 5,600ft and a height of 354ft.
Countless beaches surrounding the 38 countries in Africa make it easy for tourists and locals to enjoy surfing, scuba diving, snorkeling, kayaking or just a walk on the beach admiring the views and wildlife.
The mountains create and abundance of hiking trails through rugged terrain and lush jungles, the endless deserts give an opportunity for activities such as sandboarding and dune buggy rides and the lengthy rivers are a great attraction for white water rafters.
